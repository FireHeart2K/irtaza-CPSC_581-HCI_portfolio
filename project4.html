Project 4
Visual Procrastinator Protector

Overall Description

Group: Leslie Nguyen, Dheeraj Kumar, Mahnoud Abdelaty, Vardhini Pawar &Irtaza Ansari (Myself)

As students approach final season we start to procrastinate as the deadlines keep piling on and on. Its easy to avoid work that scares us. As abled bodied we think this the stress is damaging but thinking about students with additional impairments dealing with the same amount of stress leads us to think more about our privileges.
That is why our group wanted to create a machine that could solve everyday problems for students during the studying season in a way that helps the hearing impaired.

From this, our group created the Visual Procrastinator Protector for students! It is a machine that taking in input from a keypad indicating how many minutes a student would want to study for (valid input is 1 to 9). The machine will then light up its LED for the registered number of minutes inputted. When the light is turned on the student will study and when the light turns off the student knows to take a break. Thus, turning into a fun accountability tool to motivate students!

Write-up for the concept sketches

Exploring the different inputs and output accessories for the Arduino many of the ideas surround a different type of sensor to give a user a result. Sketches 2 and 5 depend on weight inputs and sound outputs to create their meaning to a user. Sketches 8 and 9 have the servo to create meaningful movement for users to fix their everyday problems.

In the end we as a group collaborated on our initial ideas and went with a visual timer tool, we named Visual Procrastinator Protector after being given the input matrix after our other choices were taken.
